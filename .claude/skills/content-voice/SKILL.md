# Content voice

Technical, specific, first person, show-don't-tell. The audience is never
named; the content's specificity does the targeting.

## Rules

- **Every claim is backed** by a project, a number, or a link. If a sentence
  can't be backed, cut it or make it concrete.
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "21× speedup on 32 cores" beats "highly
  optimized". Metrics always carry context (baseline, hardware, dataset).
- **Banned:** "passionate", "cutting-edge", "seamless", "leverage" (verb),
  "innovative", "world-class", "delve", exclamation marks, and any sentence
  that could appear on a thousand other portfolios.
- **Honest difficulty.** "Why it's technically hard" sections name the
  actual problem (sensor latency vs. dynamics, load imbalance, confounded
  price sensitivity) — never "this was challenging".
- **Honest limitations.** "What I'd do next" is real engineering judgment,
  not humility theater. If a metric needs a caveat (e.g. MAPE vs. a naive
  baseline), the caveat is stated plainly.
- Short sentences. Active voice. Cut the first draft by a third.
- Microcopy (buttons, nav, empty states) is plain and unclever:
  "View project", not "Dive in".

## Facts

Biography, dates, metrics, links, and affiliations come only from
`docs/drafts/` + Shao's confirmations (`docs/drafts/REVIEW.md`). A
placeholder never ships; a guess never ships.
